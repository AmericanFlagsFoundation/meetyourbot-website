## The After-Hours Lead Problem Is Bigger Than You Think

Here are three numbers you need to know.

**42%** of legal searches happen after 5pm. Not during business hours. Not when your receptionist is at the front desk. In the evening, on weekends, and during the kind of late-night moments when people are scared, overwhelmed, and actively searching for help.

**70%** of those people won't leave a voicemail. Think about your own behavior. When you call someone at 11pm and hit voicemail, do you leave a message? Most people don't. They hang up and try the next option.

**80%** of potential legal clients hire the first firm that responds to them. Not the best firm. Not the most experienced firm. The first one to make contact.

Do the math. Nearly half of your potential clients are searching when you're not available. Most of them won't leave a message. And the vast majority will hire whoever gets back to them first.

The firm getting those cases right now isn't necessarily better than yours. They just have someone picking up.

And that number compounds. Because clients refer other clients. The person who hired the other firm instead of yours? Their sister-in-law might need a lawyer next year. You'll miss her too.

## How a Bot Fixes It — Without Replacing Anyone

Here's what I want to make clear: a chatbot on your website isn't a replacement for your attorneys, your paralegals, or your receptionist. It's an addition. A team member who works the shift no one else wants.

When someone visits your website at 11pm and starts a chat, your bot is there. It introduces itself with your firm's name and branding. It answers the most common questions: What practice areas do you handle? Do you work on contingency? How does the consultation process work? What should I do right now if I was just in an accident?

If the visitor is a good lead, the bot collects their name, phone number, and a brief description of their situation. It tells them a member of the team will follow up first thing in the morning — or if they're urgent, it offers to page the on-call attorney.

And here's the part that changes everything: the moment that lead comes in, your phone buzzes. Not an email. A real-time push notification with the person's name, their case type, and a link to the conversation. You can respond in five minutes or wait until morning. But you know. You never miss it.
